Tornadoes in USA 2018

The USA is the tornado capital of the world, having by far the most tornadoes in the world. In 2018 alone, there were 1135 tornadoes throughout the year whereas Australia only have roughly 10 to 20 annually.

This map shows all the tornadoes that affected the USA in the year of 2018, including details of Tornado width and property damage costs.

TORNADO ALLEY

Tornado Alley is the name given to a group of neighbouring states where most of the tornados in the country occur. This is because of their location as hot and cool winds more frequently meet to eventually form tornados.

Tornado Alley can be seen on the map above where the tornadoes formed are more dense.

The group of states include Iowa(IA), Kansas(KS), Louisiana(LA), Nebraska(NE), Oklahoma(OK), South Dakota(SD) and Texas(TX).

When filtering to a state, groups of circles in the form of approximate vertical lines become more visible on the chart. This is because tornadoes often form in clusters known as an outbreak. All outbreaks that formed within Tornado Alley can be observed in this chart.

Texas seems to have the most tornadoes in the country for most of the years from 1950-2015, all this does not happen to be the case in more recent years.

TORNADO SEASON

Tornado season in the US is typically between March an June, meaning that tornado formation is concentrated within that timeframe and much more scarce outside it.
